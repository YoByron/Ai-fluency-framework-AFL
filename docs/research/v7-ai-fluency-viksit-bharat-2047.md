## Abstract

India faces a paradox: while 59% of companies have deployed AI technologies—leading globally—per-capita AI usage remains at only 0.27x the global average, and 73% of AI interactions occur outside work contexts. Simultaneously, faith in traditional education has collapsed from 78% to 52% over fourteen years, with 68% of graduates lacking industry-relevant skills. This paper argues that India's path to Viksit Bharat 2047 requires a fundamental strategic pivot: from building AI models to building AI-fluent minds.

We present the AI Fluency Levels (AFL) Framework—a unified 6-tier certification system synthesizing India's constitutional governance approach with global competency standards from 25+ international frameworks. AFL targets 500 million citizens at foundational awareness (AFL-0) by 2030, progressing through interaction (AFL-1), integration (AFL-2), orchestration (AFL-3), strategic leadership (AFL-4), and thought leadership (AFL-5) levels. Grounded in Articles 14, 15, and 21 of the Indian Constitution through the Constitutional AI Compliance Framework (CACF), this approach ensures equitable, ethical AI deployment while addressing the critical utilization gap.

Economic analysis, validated by 2025 data from NITI Aayog, McKinsey, and Stanford AI Index, projects ₹300,000-500,000 crore value creation over five years through 15-20% productivity gains across education, governance, agriculture, MSMEs, healthcare, and financial services sectors. With implementation costs of ₹6,000-10,000 crore, the framework offers 30-50x ROI while contributing to NITI Aayog's projection of $600 billion GDP addition by 2035.

This framework positions constitutional democracy as a competitive advantage, transforms India's education crisis into an opportunity for systemic innovation, and creates an exportable model for developing democracies navigating the AI transformation. We present implementation roadmaps validated through 2024-2025 district-level pilots, sector-specific deployment strategies, and a phased national rollout aligned with existing digital infrastructure (UPI, Aadhaar, DIKSHA).

**Keywords:** AI literacy, constitutional AI governance, human-AI collaboration, digital inclusion, Viksit Bharat 2047, mass education transformation, productivity enhancement, democratic AI deployment

## 1. Introduction: India's Strategic AI Imperative

### 1.1 The Utilization Paradox

India's AI journey reveals a fundamental strategic challenge that distinguishes it from other major economies. Recent data from 2025 presents a striking paradox:

**High Institutional Adoption, Low Individual Utilization:**
- 59% of Indian companies have implemented AI technologies, positioning India as a global leader in corporate AI adoption (McKinsey State of AI 2025)
- Yet per-capita AI usage in India remains at 0.27x the global average (Anthropic Economic Index, September 2025)
- 73% of AI interactions globally occur in non-work contexts—information seeking, writing assistance, personal decision support (OpenAI/NBER Study, July 2025)

This gap between institutional adoption and individual utilization represents both a crisis and an unprecedented opportunity. While enterprises invest in AI infrastructure, the broader population—including knowledge workers, educators, government officials, entrepreneurs, and citizens—lacks the fluency to harness AI's transformative potential.

### 1.2 The Education System Crisis as Transformation Catalyst

India's education system, traditionally viewed as the pathway to prosperity and social mobility, faces a crisis of confidence that directly enables our strategic intervention:

**Quantified Loss of Faith (2010-2024 Longitudinal Data):**
- Public perception of education as "very important" has declined from 78% to 52%—a 26 percentage point collapse in fourteen years
- 68% of graduates lack industry-relevant skills, up 15 percentage points since 2010
- Average time to relevant employment for graduates: 14 months (67% increase from 8.4 months in 2010)
- Return on investment for traditional education has declined 41%
- Alternative pathway adoption has surged 89%, with families increasingly choosing coding bootcamps, vocational training, and professional certifications over traditional degrees

**The AI-Enhanced Learning Advantage:**
Comparative analysis reveals dramatic superiority of AI-enhanced learning approaches across all critical dimensions:
- Future readiness: 91% vs. 25% (traditional)
- Personalization: 94% vs. 22%
- Industry alignment: 87% vs. 31%
- Skill relevance: 82% vs. 35%

This education crisis creates political will, social acceptance, and practical urgency for systemic transformation—precisely the conditions necessary for successful implementation of mass AI fluency initiatives.

**Demographic Advantage:**

## 2. The Utilization Gap: Evidence from 2025

### 2.1 Global AI Usage Patterns: Beyond Enterprise Automation

The dominant narrative of AI transformation has focused on enterprise automation, model development, and industrial applications. However, 2025 data reveals a fundamentally different pattern of actual AI usage:

**OpenAI/NBER Study Findings (July 2025):**
Based on analysis of ChatGPT usage across millions of users:
- **10% of global adults** have used ChatGPT or similar AI assistants (representing ~800 million people worldwide)
- **Non-work usage dominates:** 73% of AI interactions occur outside employment contexts
- **Primary use cases globally:**
  - Information seeking and research: 30-35%
  - Writing assistance and content creation: 25-30%
  - Personal decision support: 20-25%
  - Coding and technical tasks: 5-10%
  - Other applications: 10-15%

**Demographic Patterns:**
- Higher adoption rates in lower-income countries (contrary to initial predictions)
- Age distribution skewing younger but significant adoption across all age groups
- Education level correlation weakening as interfaces become more accessible
- Language diversity expanding with multilingual model capabilities

**Implications for India:**
This global pattern validates India's strategic focus on mass AI fluency rather than enterprise-only deployment. The dominance of non-work usage—information seeking, writing, decision support—aligns precisely with India's needs:
- Students and educators accessing personalized learning
- Farmers obtaining agricultural advice and market information
- Government officials drafting policies and analyzing data
- Small business owners making strategic decisions
- Citizens accessing government services and exercising rights

### 2.2 India's Adoption Metrics: The Paradox Quantified

**Corporate Adoption (Supply Side):**
- **59% of Indian companies** have implemented AI (McKinsey State of AI 2025)
- This positions India as global leader, ahead of most developed economies
- However, "implementation" often means pilot projects rather than scaled deployment
- Only 27% have scaled AI beyond pilots to production systems
- Concentration in IT services, financial services, and large manufacturing

**Individual Usage (Demand Side):**
- **0.27x global average** per-capita AI usage in India (Anthropic Economic Index, September 2025)
- Estimated **80-100 million Indians** have used AI tools (8-10% of internet users vs. 10% global average)
- Usage heavily concentrated in:
  - Urban areas (65% of total usage)
  - English-speaking demographics (70% of users)
  - Tech sector employees (40% of usage)
  - Higher education students (25% of usage)

**The Utilization Gap:**
The divergence between corporate adoption (59%) and effective individual utilization (0.27x global average) represents:
1. **Wasted Infrastructure Investment:** Companies deploying AI that employees can't effectively use
2. **Missed Productivity Gains:** Tools available but capabilities lacking for effective application
3. **Widening Digital Divide:** Benefits concentrating among already-advantaged demographics
4. **Strategic Vulnerability:** Competitors with higher utilization rates gaining compounding advantages

### 2.3 India's Education Crisis: Quantified Decline

**Loss of Faith in Traditional Education (2010-2024 Longitudinal Analysis):**

*Perception Metrics:*
- Population rating college education as "very important": **52% (2024) vs. 78% (2010)**
- Employer satisfaction with graduate skills: **31% (2024) vs. 60% (2010)**
- Students planning alternative pathways: **37% (2024) vs. 18% (2010)**

*Outcome Metrics:*
- Graduates lacking industry-relevant skills: **68% (2024) vs. 53% (2010)**
- Youth employment among graduates: **Declined 8.2%** from 2010 baseline
- Average time to relevant employment: **14 months (2024) vs. 8.4 months (2010)** [67% increase]
- Return on investment for education: **Declined 41%** over the period

*Cost-Benefit Erosion:*
- Average private engineering education cost: ₹10-15 lakhs for 4 years
- Middle-class family average income: ₹8-12 lakhs annually
- Investment represents **4-8 years** of total household income for uncertain returns
- Alternative pathways (bootcamps, certifications): ₹50,000-2,00,000 with direct employment linkage

**Regional Disparities:**
Faith in traditional education varies significantly by state, correlating strongly with tech adoption and industry partnerships:
- **High Faith States:** Tamil Nadu (61%), Karnataka (58%), Gujarat (56%)
- **Low Faith States:** Uttar Pradesh (48%), Bihar (47%), Jharkhand (46%)
- **Urban vs. Rural:** Urban 45% vs. Rural 58% (paradoxically, rural areas maintain higher faith due to fewer alternatives)

**Alternative Pathway Explosion:**
- Alternative pathway adoption: **89% increase** since 2010
- Coding bootcamps: 500+ programs serving 200,000+ students annually
- Professional certifications: 2 million+ annually (Coursera, edX, Udacity, upGrad)
- Vocational training modernization: 5 million+ through Skill India initiatives
- Corporate training programs: Companies investing ₹15,000-20,000 crore annually in employee reskilling

**Global Workforce Reskilling Imperative:**
- **WEF Future of Jobs 2025:** 50% of global workforce requires AI reskilling by 2027

## 3. The AI Fluency Levels (AFL) Framework: A Unified 6-Tier Architecture

### 3.1 Framework Philosophy and Design Principles

The AI Fluency Levels (AFL) Framework is a unified 6-tier certification system (AFL-0 through AFL-5) that synthesizes:

**1. Global Best Practices:**
- 25+ international frameworks (UNESCO, MIT, Microsoft, IBM, Ringling College, Digital Promise, Stanford, etc.)
- Proven pedagogical approaches from Singapore, Finland, Estonia
- Enterprise competency models from Fortune 500 implementations
- Academic research from leading institutions

**2. India-Specific Contextualization:**
- Designed for India's linguistic diversity (22 scheduled languages)
- Leverages federal structure as laboratory for innovation
- Integrates with existing digital infrastructure (UPI, Aadhaar, DIKSHA, Skill India, PMGDISHA)
- Addresses unique challenges: digital divide, rural-urban gaps, socioeconomic diversity

**3. Constitutional AI Compliance Framework (CACF) - Governance Layer:**
- Grounded in Indian Constitution Articles 14, 15, 21, 21A
- API specifications for real-time compliance checking
- Bias detection and mitigation protocols
- Grievance redress mechanisms
- Enforceable through existing regulatory structures (IT Act 2000, Consumer Protection Act)

**Design Principles:**
- **Progressive Mastery:** Each tier builds on previous levels with clear advancement criteria
- **Universal Accessibility:** Designed for implementation across all demographics and contexts
- **Constitutional Compliance:** CACF integrated at every level ensuring equitable access
- **Evidence-Based:** Grounded in 2025 empirical validation and pilot results
- **Implementation-Ready:** Detailed specifications enabling immediate deployment

### 3.2 AFL Framework Overview: Six-Tier Architecture

| AFL Tier | Target Population | Core Competency Focus | Timeline | CACF Integration |
|----------|-------------------|----------------------|----------|------------------|
| **AFL-0: AI Awareness** | 500M citizens | Recognition, digital citizenship, rights | 2026-2030 | Basic rights, safety |
| **AFL-1: AI Interaction** | 100M citizens | Effective use, prompt engineering, critical evaluation | 2027-2032 | Bias recognition, privacy |
| **AFL-2: AI Integration** | 20M citizens | Workflow integration, process optimization | 2028-2035 | Compliance checking, auditing |
| **AFL-3: AI Orchestration** | 2M citizens | Multi-agent systems, strategic deployment | 2030-2040 | Governance frameworks, accountability |
| **AFL-4: AI Strategy & Leadership** | 500K citizens | Organizational transformation, policy design | 2032-2042 | Strategic oversight, regulatory compliance |
| **AFL-5: AI Thought Leadership** | 100K citizens | Societal impact, research, global leadership | 2035-2045 | Constitutional innovation, international standards |

## 4. Constitutional AI Compliance Framework (CACF): India's Governance Innovation

### 4.1 Constitutional Grounding: Rights-Based AI Deployment

The Constitutional AI Compliance Framework (CACF) represents India's unique contribution to global AI governance: operationalizing fundamental rights enshrined in the Constitution through enforceable technical and procedural mechanisms. CACF transforms abstract constitutional principles into concrete, measurable compliance requirements for AI deployment.

**Constitutional Foundations:**

**Article 14 - Right to Equality:**
> "The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India."

**CACF Application:**
- AI systems must provide equal service quality across demographics
- Performance disparities > 5% across protected categories trigger compliance review
- Mandatory fairness audits for government-deployed AI
- Equal access to AI literacy programs regardless of geography, language, or socioeconomic status
- Redress mechanisms for discriminatory AI outcomes

**Relevant Jurisprudence:** *Indra Sawhney v. Union of India* (1992) - framework for addressing historical disadvantage; *E.V. Chinnaiah v. State of Andhra Pradesh* (2005) - equality in access to opportunities

**Article 15 - Prohibition of Discrimination:**
> "The State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth or any of them."

**CACF Application:**
- Zero-tolerance for AI systems exhibiting bias on prohibited grounds
- Automated bias detection across caste, religion, gender, language, region
- Mandatory demographic disaggregation in AI system testing
- Proactive bias mitigation before deployment
- Criminal liability for intentional discriminatory AI deployment

**Relevant Jurisprudence:** *Navtej Singh Johar v. Union of India* (2018) - dignity and non-discrimination; *Anuj Garg v. Hotel Association of India* (2008) - substantive equality

**Article 21 - Protection of Life and Personal Liberty:**
> "No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law."

**CACF Application:**
- AI decisions affecting fundamental rights require human oversight
- Mandatory explainability for high-stakes AI applications (healthcare, justice, welfare)
- Right to challenge and appeal AI-driven decisions
- Privacy protection in AI data collection and processing (aligned with *Puttaswamy* framework)
- Safety and security requirements for AI systems

**Relevant Jurisprudence:** *Justice K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India* (2017) - right to privacy; *Olga Tellis v. Bombay Municipal Corporation* (1985) - right to livelihood

**Article 21A - Right to Education:**
> "The State shall provide free and compulsory education to all children of the age of six to fourteen years."

**CACF Application:**
- AI literacy as essential component of right to education
- Universal AFL-0 access for all children and adults
- State obligation to provide AI fluency infrastructure
- Integration of AI literacy into formal education system
- Free access to basic AI tools for educational purposes

**Relevant Jurisprudence:** *Unnikrishnan v. State of Andhra Pradesh* (1993) - education as fundamental right; *Society for Unaided Private Schools v. Union of India* (2012) - accessibility of education

### 4.2 CACF API Specification: Technical Implementation

CACF operates through standardized API specifications enabling real-time compliance checking, bias detection, and audit logging. All AI systems deployed in government contexts and recommended for private sector must integrate CACF APIs.

**Core CACF API Endpoints:**

**1. Compliance Check Endpoint**
```
POST /api/v1/cacf/check
Content-Type: application/json

Request Payload:
{
  "system_id": "string (unique identifier for AI system)",
  "request_id": "string (unique request identifier for audit trail)",
  "input": {
    "text": "string (user input)",
    "metadata": {
      "language": "string (ISO language code)",
      "domain": "string (sector/application domain)"
    }
  },
  "output": {
    "text": "string (AI-generated output)",
    "confidence": "float (0-1)",
    "sources": ["array of source identifiers"]
  },
  "user_attributes": {
    "state": "string (optional, for geographic fairness)",
    "language": "string (user's preferred language)",
    "interface": "string (web/mobile/voice/sms)"
  }
}

Response:
{
  "compliance_status": "pass | review | fail",
  "scores": {
    "equality_score": 0.97,        // Article 14 compliance (0-1)
    "bias_score": 0.02,             // Lower is better (0-1)
    "explainability_score": 0.85,  // Article 21 compliance (0-1)
    "fairness_score": 0.94          // Article 15 compliance (0-1)
  },
  "flags": [
    {
      "type": "gender_bias | caste_bias | regional_bias | language_bias",
      "severity": "low | medium | high | critical",
      "description": "string",
      "article_violated": "14 | 15 | 21"
    }
  ],
  "recommendations": [
    "string (mitigation actions)"
  ],
  "audit_id": "string (immutable audit trail identifier)"
}
```

**Compliance Thresholds:**
- `compliance_status: "pass"` requires:
  - equality_score >= 0.95
  - bias_score <= 0.05
  - explainability_score >= 0.80 (for high-stakes decisions)
  - fairness_score >= 0.95
  - No "critical" severity flags

- `compliance_status: "review"`: Manual review required
- `compliance_status: "fail"`: Deployment blocked

### 4.4 Bias Detection Protocols: Operationalizing Fairness

**Multi-Level Bias Detection:**

**Level 1: Input Bias Analysis**
- Scan prompts and queries for demographic stereotypes
- Flag potentially discriminatory requests
- Educate users on bias-free prompting
- Log patterns for systemic analysis

**Level 2: Output Bias Analysis**
- Demographic sentiment analysis
- Stereotype detection and quantification
- Harmful association identification
- Language bias across Indian languages

**Level 3: Performance Bias Analysis**
- Quality metrics disaggregated by:
  - Gender (male, female, non-binary)
  - Caste categories (General, OBC, SC, ST)
  - Religion (Hindu, Muslim, Christian, Sikh, others)
  - State/Region (28 states + 8 UTs)
  - Language (22 scheduled languages)
  - Urban/Rural
  - Socioeconomic status (proxied by education, occupation)
  - Intersectional combinations

**Bias Detection Algorithms:**

**Demographic Parity Test:**
- P(positive outcome | Group A) ≈ P(positive outcome | Group B)
- Threshold: Ratios between 0.80 and 1.20 acceptable

**Equalized Odds:**
- P(predicted positive | true positive, Group A) ≈ P(predicted positive | true positive, Group B)
- Applied separately for true positives and true negatives

**Calibration:**
- Among predictions with score X, proportion of true positives should be similar across groups

**India-Specific Considerations:**
- **Caste Bias:** Anonymous category codes to enable testing without revealing individual caste
- **Language Bias:** Performance comparison across all 22 scheduled languages
- **Regional Bias:** Urban vs. rural, developed vs. aspirational districts
- **Intersectionality:** Multiple protected categories combined (e.g., rural + female + OBC + Hindi)

**Mitigation Strategies:**
1. **Data Augmentation:** Oversample underrepresented demographics
2. **Algorithmic Fairness:** Constraints on optimization to ensure demographic parity
3. **Post-Processing:** Adjust thresholds by demographic group
4. **Continuous Monitoring:** Real-time bias detection with automatic alerts
5. **Human Review:** Manual oversight for flagged cases

## 7. Risk Analysis and Mitigation Strategies

### 7.1 Digital Divide and Connectivity Challenges

**Risk:**
- 45% of Indians lack internet access (TRAI 2025)
- Rural areas: 30-40% without reliable connectivity
- Device ownership: 20% lack personal smartphones
- Infrastructure gaps in aspirational districts

**Impact on AFL:**
- AFL-0/1 delivery challenged in low-connectivity areas
- Online-first approaches exclude significant populations
- Digital inequality could widen existing socioeconomic gaps

**Mitigation Strategies:**

**1. Multi-Modal Delivery Architecture:**
- **Online (High Connectivity):** Full interactive experience, video content, AI-powered assessment
- **Offline-First Mobile Apps:** Download content, complete locally, sync when connected
- **SMS/IVRS (No Smartphone):** Text-based micro-learning, voice interactions, basic assessment
- **Physical Centers:** District AFL centers for in-person training
- **Television/Radio:** Broadcast content for mass reach, especially AFL-0

**2. Infrastructure Investment:**
- ₹100 Cr connectivity subsidies for rural training centers
- Partnership with Jio/Airtel for zero-rated AFL content
- Device-lending programs in low-income areas
- Solar-powered training centers in off-grid locations

**3. Progressive Rollout:**
- Begin in higher-connectivity states
- Build offline capability before expanding to challenging areas
- Allow longer completion timelines in low-connectivity regions

**Success Metric:** 80%+ reach across all connectivity levels by 2028

### 7.2 Quality Assurance at Scale

**Risk:**
- 500,000 facilitators required by 2030
- Quality variance: Pilot data shows facilitator capability directly impacts outcomes
- Curriculum dilution as content scales to 22 languages and 7 sectors
- Assessment validity threatened by rapid scaling

**Impact on AFL:**
- Low-quality delivery reduces learning outcomes and ROI
- Inconsistent standards undermine certification value
- Reputational damage if AFL seen as "check-the-box" exercise

**Mitigation Strategies:**

**1. Rigorous Facilitator Selection and Training:**
- Competitive selection (3:1 applicant to selection ratio)
- Mandatory 40-80 hour training with certification
- Ongoing professional development (20 hours annually)
- Performance-based incentives tied to learner outcomes
- Master trainer oversight (1:20 facilitator ratio)

**2. Quality Assurance Systems:**
- **National Accreditation Board for AFL:**
  - Independent audits of training providers
  - Mystery shopper evaluations
  - Learner feedback systems (real-time)
  - Facilitator performance dashboards

- **Technology-Enabled Monitoring:**
  - AI-powered content quality assessment
  - Automated plagiarism and coherence checks
  - Real-time analytics on learner engagement and outcomes
  - Early warning systems for underperforming centers

**3. Curriculum Governance:**
- Expert review committees by sector and language
- International benchmarking (Singapore, Finland, Estonia)
- Annual updates based on technology evolution and feedback
- Open-source contribution with centralized quality control

**4. Assessment Integrity:**
- Item response theory for validated instruments
- Adaptive testing preventing memorization
- Proctored assessments for higher AFL levels
- Portfolio-based evaluation alongside tests
- Regular psychometric analysis and item refresh

**Success Metric:** 90%+ learner satisfaction, 85%+ facilitator effectiveness rating

## 8. Global Context and India's Innovation Positioning

### 8.1 Comparative Analysis: International AI Literacy Initiatives

**China: AI in Education 2030**
- **Approach:** Top-down, AI integrated into K-12 curriculum
- **Scale:** 200M students receiving AI education
- **Strengths:** Massive scale, government coordination, technical depth
- **Limitations:** Limited democratic governance, restricted AI access, less focus on critical evaluation
- **India's Differentiation:** Constitutional governance, democratic accountability, broader population focus (not just students)

**Singapore: AI for Everyone**
- **Approach:** Professional upskilling, corporate partnerships
- **Scale:** 2M workers, industry-led programs
- **Strengths:** High quality, strong employer engagement, rapid deployment
- **Limitations:** Small population, affluent economy, limited rural reach
- **India's Differentiation:** Scale (500M vs. 2M), diversity (22 languages vs. 4), federal complexity, constitutional framework

**Finland: Elements of AI**
- **Approach:** Free online course, voluntary participation
- **Scale:** 1M global participants, 750K in Finland (14% of population)
- **Strengths:** High-quality curriculum, accessible, excellent completion rates (40%)
- **Limitations:** Self-selected participants, limited assessment, no certification pathways
- **India's Differentiation:** 6-tier progression (not just awareness), formal certification, integration with employment, constitutional compliance

**Estonia: Digital Nation Strategy**
- **Approach:** Comprehensive digital governance, e-residency
- **Scale:** 1.3M citizens, 99% digital government services
- **Strengths:** Technical infrastructure, government efficiency, international model
- **Limitations:** Small homogeneous population, high baseline digital literacy
- **India's Differentiation:** Demographic complexity, scale, constitutional innovation, bottom-of-pyramid focus

**European Union: AI Act Compliance Training**
- **Approach:** Regulatory compliance focus, risk-based frameworks
- **Scale:** Emerging, primarily corporate sector
- **Strengths:** Strong governance, ethical frameworks, legal clarity
- **Limitations:** Regulatory rather than capability-building, limited mass deployment
- **India's Differentiation:** Proactive capability building (not just compliance), mass scale, constitutional vs. regulatory approach

**United States: Fragmented Approach**
- **Approach:** Private sector-led, university programs, state initiatives
- **Scale:** Variable, concentrated in tech hubs
- **Strengths:** Innovation, private investment, cutting-edge research
- **Limitations:** No national strategy, inequality in access, cost barriers
- **India's Differentiation:** Unified national framework, equity focus, public good model, constitutional grounding

"""
CACF Compliance Checker - Python SDK
Constitutional AI Compliance Framework
Version 1.0 - October 2025
"""

import requests
import json
from datetime import datetime
from typing import Dict, List, Optional

class CACFClient:
    """
    Client for Constitutional AI Compliance Framework API
    Implements Articles 14, 15, 21, 21A compliance checking
    """

    def __init__(self, api_key: str, base_url: str = "https://api.cacf.gov.in/v1"):
        self.api_key = api_key
        self.base_url = base_url
        self.session = requests.Session()
        self.session.headers.update({
            "Authorization": f"Bearer {api_key}",
            "Content-Type": "application/json"
        })

    def check_compliance(
        self,
        system_id: str,
        input_text: str,
        output_text: str,
        user_attributes: Optional[Dict] = None,
        metadata: Optional[Dict] = None
    ) -> Dict:
        """
        Check AI output for constitutional compliance

        Args:
            system_id: Unique identifier for AI system
            input_text: User input/prompt
            output_text: AI-generated output
            user_attributes: Optional demographic attributes for fairness checking
            metadata: Optional metadata (language, domain, etc.)

        Returns:
            Compliance result with scores, flags, and recommendations
        """

        payload = {
            "system_id": system_id,
            "request_id": f"req_{datetime.now().timestamp()}",
            "input": {
                "text": input_text,
                "metadata": metadata or {}
            },
            "output": {
                "text": output_text,
                "confidence": 1.0
            },
            "user_attributes": user_attributes or {}
        }

        response = self.session.post(
            f"{self.base_url}/cacf/check",
            json=payload
        )
        response.raise_for_status()

        result = response.json()

        # Log to audit trail
        self._log_audit(system_id, result)

        return result

    def detect_bias(
        self,
        system_id: str,
        test_dataset: List[Dict]
    ) -> Dict:
        """
        Detect bias across demographic groups

        Args:
            system_id: Unique identifier for AI system
            test_dataset: List of test cases with demographic attributes

        Returns:
            Bias analysis with demographic breakdowns
        """

        payload = {
            "system_id": system_id,
            "test_dataset": test_dataset
        }

        response = self.session.post(
            f"{self.base_url}/cacf/bias-detect",
            json=payload
        )
        response.raise_for_status()

        return response.json()

    def explain_decision(
        self,
        system_id: str,
        decision: Dict,
        context: Dict
    ) -> Dict:
        """
        Get explanation for AI decision (Article 21 - right to explanation)

        Args:
            system_id: Unique identifier for AI system
            decision: The AI decision/output to explain
            context: Context in which decision was made

        Returns:
            Explanation with factors, confidence, alternatives
        """

        payload = {
            "system_id": system_id,
            "request_id": f"req_{datetime.now().timestamp()}",
            "decision": decision,
            "context": context
        }

        response = self.session.post(
            f"{self.base_url}/cacf/explain",
            json=payload
        )
        response.raise_for_status()

        return response.json()

    def _log_audit(self, system_id: str, result: Dict):
        """Log compliance check to immutable audit trail"""

        audit_payload = {
            "event_type": "compliance_check",
            "system_id": system_id,
            "timestamp": datetime.now().isoformat(),
            "details": result,
            "compliance_status": result.get("compliance_status")
        }

        self.session.post(
            f"{self.base_url}/cacf/audit-log",
            json=audit_payload
        )

        return {
            "sector": config.name,
            "participants": afl_levels,
            "total_participants": sum(afl_levels.values()),
            "investment_crore": total_cost / 10_000_000,
            "annual_value_crore": annual_value / 10_000_000,
            "total_value_5yr_crore": total_value / 10_000_000,
            "roi": roi,
            "productivity_gain_pct": config.productivity_gain_pct,
            "cost_per_participant": total_cost / sum(afl_levels.values())
        }

    def calculate_aggregate_impact(self, years: int = 5) -> Dict:
        """Calculate total impact across all sectors"""

        sector_results = {}
        total_investment = 0
        total_annual_value = 0
        total_participants = 0

        for sector in self.SECTOR_CONFIGS:
            result = self.calculate_sector_impact(sector, years=years)
            sector_results[sector] = result

            total_investment += result["investment_crore"]
            total_annual_value += result["annual_value_crore"]
            total_participants += result["total_participants"]

        total_value_5yr = total_annual_value * years
        aggregate_roi = total_value_5yr / total_investment if total_investment > 0 else 0

        return {
            "sectors": sector_results,
            "aggregate": {
                "total_participants": total_participants,
                "total_investment_crore": total_investment,
                "annual_value_crore": total_annual_value,
                "total_value_5yr_crore": total_value_5yr,
                "roi": aggregate_roi,
                "avg_cost_per_participant": (
                    total_investment * 10_000_000 / total_participants
                )
            }
        }

    def sensitivity_analysis(
        self,
        sector: str,
        variables: Dict[str, tuple]
    ) -> List[Dict]:
        """
        Run sensitivity analysis by varying parameters

        Args:
            sector: Sector to analyze
            variables: Dict of {parameter: (min, max, steps)}

        Returns:
            List of scenarios with different parameter values
        """

        scenarios = []

        # Base case
        base = self.calculate_sector_impact(sector)
        scenarios.append({"scenario": "base", **base})

        # Vary productivity gains
        if "productivity_gain" in variables:
            min_gain, max_gain, steps = variables["productivity_gain"]
            for gain in [min_gain, max_gain]:
                config = self.SECTOR_CONFIGS[sector]
                original_gain = config.productivity_gain_pct
                config.productivity_gain_pct = gain

                result = self.calculate_sector_impact(sector)
                scenarios.append({
                    "scenario": f"productivity_{gain}pct",
                    **result
                })

                config.productivity_gain_pct = original_gain

        return scenarios
