# District Pilot Playbook: Comprehensive Implementation Guide

**Version:** 2.0  
**Last Updated:** November 2025  
**Purpose:** Enable district administrators to launch AFL pilots with clear, actionable guidance

---

## Executive Summary

This playbook provides step-by-step guidance for implementing AI Fluency Levels (AFL) pilots at the district level. Based on validated pilots across 5 diverse districts (Bangalore Urban, Lucknow, Thrissur, Kalahandi, Dantewada) and aligned with the v7 research framework for Viksit Bharat 2047.

**Expected Outcomes:**
- 10,000-50,000 citizens certified at AFL-0 (6-12 months)
- 1,000-5,000 citizens certified at AFL-1 (12-18 months)
- 100-500 citizens certified at AFL-2 (18-24 months)
- Measurable 8-18% productivity gains in target sectors
- 90%+ CACF compliance in government AI systems

**Investment Required:** ₹50 lakhs - ₹2 crore (depending on district size and scope)

## 4. Infrastructure Setup

### 4.1 Digital Platform

**Required Components:**

1. **Learning Management System (LMS)**
   - Recommended: DIKSHA integration (free, government-supported)
   - Alternative: Moodle, Google Classroom (if DIKSHA unavailable)
   - Features needed:
     - User registration and authentication
     - Course delivery (video, text, interactive)
     - Progress tracking
     - Assessment and certification
     - Multilingual support
     - Offline capability

2. **Mobile Application**
   - Progressive Web App (PWA) for broad compatibility
   - Android app (70% of users)
   - iOS app (if budget permits)
   - SMS/IVRS for feature phones (rural areas)

3. **Dashboard & Analytics**
   - Real-time enrollment and completion tracking
   - Demographic breakdowns
   - Geographic visualization
   - Facilitator performance metrics
   - CACF compliance monitoring

**Setup Timeline:** 4-6 weeks  
**Cost:** ₹5-10 lakhs (one-time) + ₹50,000/month (hosting, maintenance)

### 4.2 Physical Infrastructure

**Learning Centers:**

**Urban Areas:**
- Leverage existing: Schools (after hours), colleges, PMGDISHA centers, libraries
- Target: 1 center per 50,000 population
- Requirements: 20-30 computers, projector, internet, facilitator

**Rural Areas:**
- Leverage: Panchayat offices, community centers, schools
- Target: 1 center per 10,000 population
- Requirements: 10-15 computers, projector, internet (or offline content), facilitator

**Equipment Checklist (per center):**
- [ ] Computers/laptops (20-30 for urban, 10-15 for rural)
- [ ] Projector and screen
- [ ] Internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps)
- [ ] Backup power (UPS/generator)
- [ ] Furniture (tables, chairs)
- [ ] Whiteboard/flipchart
- [ ] Audio system (speakers)

### 4.3 Connectivity Solutions

**For Low-Connectivity Areas:**

1. **Offline Content Packages**
   - Pre-loaded on USB drives or SD cards
   - Distributed through facilitators
   - Periodic sync when connectivity available

2. **SMS-Based Learning**
   - Micro-lessons via SMS (160 characters)
   - Daily tips and quizzes
   - Assessment via USSD codes

3. **Voice-Based Learning**
   - IVR system for audio lessons
   - Toll-free number for access
   - Regional language support

4. **Mobile Data Partnerships**
   - Negotiate with telecom operators for zero-rated AFL content
   - Subsidized data packs for participants

## 5. Facilitator Recruitment & Training

### 5.1 Recruitment Strategy

**Target Sources:**

1. **School Teachers** (40% of facilitators)
   - Advantages: Teaching experience, community trust, existing infrastructure
   - Recruitment: Through District Education Officer
   - Incentive: Professional development, honorarium (₹20,000-30,000)

2. **Government IT Trainers** (20%)
   - Advantages: Technical skills, government network
   - Recruitment: Through PMGDISHA, Skill India centers
   - Incentive: Skill enhancement, recognition

3. **Corporate Volunteers** (20%)
   - Advantages: Industry expertise, real-world examples
   - Recruitment: Through CSR partnerships
   - Incentive: CSR credits, community impact

4. **Civil Society** (20%)
   - Advantages: Community reach, grassroots experience
   - Recruitment: Through NGO partnerships
   - Incentive: Capacity building, program funding

**Selection Criteria:**
- Minimum qualification: Bachelor's degree
- Digital literacy: Comfortable with computers, smartphones
- Communication skills: Ability to explain concepts clearly
- Language: Fluency in local language(s)
- Commitment: Available for 3-6 months (part-time or full-time)
- Preferred: AFL-1 or AFL-2 certified (or willing to get certified)

### 5.2 Facilitator Training Program

**AFL-0 Facilitator Training (40 hours over 1 week):**

**Day 1-2: Content Mastery (16 hours)**
- Module 1: What is AI? (4 hours)
- Module 2: AI in Indian Life (4 hours)
- Module 3: Rights and Safety (CACF) (4 hours)
- Module 4: Future Readiness (4 hours)

**Day 3-4: Pedagogy & Facilitation (16 hours)**
- Adult learning principles (4 hours)
- Facilitation techniques (4 hours)
- Handling diverse learners (4 hours)
- Assessment and feedback (4 hours)

**Day 5: Practice & Certification (8 hours)**
- Mock sessions (4 hours)
- Peer feedback (2 hours)
- Certification assessment (2 hours)

**AFL-1 Facilitator Training (80 hours over 2 weeks):**
- Week 1: AFL-1 content mastery + AFL-0 facilitation skills
- Week 2: Advanced prompting, critical evaluation, sector-specific modules, hands-on practice

**Ongoing Support:**
- Monthly facilitator community calls
- WhatsApp/Telegram group for peer support
- Quarterly refresher workshops
- Recognition and awards for top performers

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide

### 9.1 Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: Low Enrollment**

**Symptoms:**
- Enrollment below 50% of target
- Slow growth in registrations
- Low awareness in community

**Root Causes:**
- Insufficient marketing and outreach
- Lack of trust or understanding of benefits
- Inconvenient timing or locations
- Language barriers

**Solutions:**
- ✅ Intensify marketing: Local media, influencers, door-to-door campaigns
- ✅ Leverage existing networks: Schools, panchayats, self-help groups
- ✅ Showcase early success stories
- ✅ Offer flexible timings and locations
- ✅ Provide incentives: Certificates, job placement support, prizes
- ✅ Ensure content in local language

**Challenge 2: High Drop-Out Rate**

**Symptoms:**
- Completion rate below 40%
- Participants dropping after initial modules
- Irregular attendance

**Root Causes:**
- Content too difficult or not relevant
- Time constraints (work, family)
- Technical issues (connectivity, devices)
- Lack of motivation or support

**Solutions:**
- ✅ Simplify content, add more examples
- ✅ Offer flexible, self-paced learning
- ✅ Provide offline options
- ✅ Strengthen facilitator support
- ✅ Create peer learning groups
- ✅ Offer micro-credentials for module completion
- ✅ Address technical issues promptly

**Challenge 3: Poor Learning Outcomes**

**Symptoms:**
- Low assessment scores
- Participants unable to apply learning
- Negative feedback on content quality

**Root Causes:**
- Content not aligned with participant needs
- Poor facilitation quality
- Insufficient practice opportunities
- Language or cultural barriers

**Solutions:**
- ✅ Revise content based on feedback
- ✅ Provide additional facilitator training
- ✅ Increase hands-on practice sessions
- ✅ Localize examples and case studies
- ✅ Offer remedial support for struggling learners
- ✅ Peer mentoring programs

**Challenge 4: Connectivity Issues**

**Symptoms:**
- Participants unable to access online content
- Frequent disconnections during sessions
- Rural participants excluded

**Root Causes:**
- Poor internet infrastructure
- Lack of devices
- High data costs

**Solutions:**
- ✅ Offline content packages (USB, SD cards)
- ✅ SMS/voice-based learning
- ✅ Learning centers with reliable connectivity
- ✅ Partner with telecoms for zero-rated content
- ✅ Device lending programs
- ✅ Download-and-sync model

**Challenge 5: Facilitator Quality Issues**

**Symptoms:**
- Inconsistent delivery quality
- Participant complaints about facilitators
- Low facilitator engagement

**Root Causes:**
- Insufficient training
- Lack of ongoing support
- Low motivation or compensation
- Unclear expectations

**Solutions:**
- ✅ Enhanced facilitator training
- ✅ Regular refresher sessions
- ✅ Peer learning community
- ✅ Performance-based incentives
- ✅ Clear facilitator handbook and guidelines
- ✅ Recognition and awards program

**Challenge 6: Limited Impact Measurement**

**Symptoms:**
- Difficulty demonstrating productivity gains
- Anecdotal evidence only
- Stakeholder skepticism

**Root Causes:**
- Weak baseline data
- No systematic follow-up
- Productivity metrics not defined
- Attribution challenges

**Solutions:**
- ✅ Robust baseline surveys
- ✅ Structured follow-up at 3, 6, 12 months
- ✅ Define clear productivity metrics by sector
- ✅ Control group comparison (where feasible)
- ✅ Detailed case studies with before/after data
- ✅ Third-party evaluation

## 10. Success Stories & Templates

### 10.1 Pilot Success Stories

**Case Study 1: Bangalore Urban - Tech Sector Productivity**

**Context:** 15,000 knowledge workers from IT companies completed AFL-1

**Intervention:**
- 40-hour online course over 8 weeks
- Sector-specific modules on coding, documentation, project management
- Corporate partnerships for facilitation

**Results:**
- 72% completion rate
- 18% average productivity improvement (measured via task completion time)
- 95% participants using AI tools daily (up from 30%)
- ROI: ₹420 per participant cost, ₹12,000 productivity value per participant

**Key Success Factor:** Strong corporate buy-in, relevant use cases, online-first delivery

---

**Case Study 2: Thrissur - Agricultural Transformation**

**Context:** 5,000 farmers completed AFL-1 with agriculture focus

**Intervention:**
- 40-hour hybrid course (online + in-person)
- Malayalam content
- Agricultural AI tools: weather, pest ID, market prices
- Facilitators from Krishi Vigyan Kendra

**Results:**
- 68% completion rate
- 15% income increase (via better crop planning and market access)
- 80% using AI for weather forecasting
- 60% using AI for pest identification

**Key Success Factor:** Local language, relevant tools, trusted facilitators, community-based delivery

---

**Case Study 3: Kalahandi - Overcoming Connectivity Challenges**

**Context:** 22,000 citizens in low-connectivity rural area completed AFL-0

**Intervention:**
- Offline content packages (USB drives)
- SMS-based micro-lessons
- Community center-based sessions
- Odia language content

**Results:**
- 48% completion rate (lower but significant given challenges)
- 90% participants reported increased AI awareness
- 40% able to use basic AI tools (voice assistants, translation)
- Foundation for AFL-1 rollout

**Key Success Factor:** Offline-first approach, SMS support, community mobilization, patient timeline
