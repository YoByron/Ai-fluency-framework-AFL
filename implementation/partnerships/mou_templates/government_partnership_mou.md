## 1. BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

**WHEREAS** the Government of India has launched the AI Fluency Levels (AFL) Framework to build mass AI literacy aligned with the Viksit Bharat 2047 vision;

**WHEREAS** the AFL Framework aims to certify 500 million citizens at AFL-0 by 2030, with progressive advancement to higher levels;

**WHEREAS** the Constitutional AI Compliance Framework (CACF) ensures equitable, rights-based AI deployment in accordance with Articles 14, 15, 21, and 21A of the Indian Constitution;

**WHEREAS** Government Partner A is responsible for [national policy framework / state-level coordination / funding];

**WHEREAS** Government Partner B is responsible for [state-level implementation / district-level coordination / ground-level delivery];

## 9. CONSTITUTIONAL AI COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK (CACF)

### 9.1 CACF Commitment

Both Parties commit to upholding CACF principles:
- **Article 14 (Equality):** Equal access and service quality across demographics
- **Article 15 (Non-Discrimination):** Zero tolerance for bias on caste, religion, gender, language, region
- **Article 21 (Life and Liberty):** Privacy protection, explainability, right to challenge AI decisions
- **Article 21A (Right to Education):** Universal access to AI literacy

### 9.2 CACF Implementation

- Integration of CACF APIs in all government AI systems
- Mandatory bias audits before deployment
- Grievance redress mechanism for CACF violations
- Public reporting of CACF compliance rates

### 9.3 Equity Assurance

- Proactive outreach to underserved communities
- Multilingual content in all scheduled languages
- Offline and low-connectivity solutions
- Scholarships and fee waivers for economically disadvantaged
- Accessible design for persons with disabilities
