The objectives of this partnership are to:

1. **Inclusive Reach:** Certify [Number] citizens from underserved communities in AFL levels [0/1/2] by [Date]

## 7. COMMUNITY MOBILIZATION STRATEGY

### 7.1 Outreach and Awareness

**Activities:**
- Community meetings and awareness sessions
- Door-to-door campaigns (if needed)
- Posters, banners, and local announcements
- Engagement with community leaders and influencers
- Leveraging existing NGO programs and beneficiaries

**Messaging:**
- Benefits of AI literacy for livelihood, empowerment, access to services
- Free program with certification
- Culturally appropriate and locally relevant examples
- Addressing fears and misconceptions about AI

### 7.2 Participant Recruitment

**Target Groups:**
- Self-Help Groups (SHGs) and women's collectives
- Farmers' cooperatives and agricultural groups
- Youth clubs and student organizations
- Persons with disabilities and their support groups
- Urban slum communities and informal workers
- Tribal communities and remote villages

**Recruitment Process:**
1. Identify target groups through NGO networks
2. Conduct orientation sessions
3. Collect participant information and consent
4. Enroll in AFL platform
5. Form learning cohorts (15-25 participants)

### 7.3 Participant Support

**Barriers Addressed:**
- **Time constraints:** Flexible scheduling, weekend/evening sessions
- **Childcare:** On-site childcare or childcare allowance
- **Transportation:** Local venues, travel allowance if needed
- **Digital access:** Device lending, shared devices, offline content
- **Literacy:** Simplified content, visual learning, voice-based tools
- **Language:** Local language/dialect content and facilitation
- **Confidence:** Safe learning spaces, peer support, patient facilitation

## 12. CONSTITUTIONAL AI COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK (CACF)

### 12.1 CACF Commitment

Both Parties commit to upholding CACF principles:
- **Article 14 (Equality):** Equal access and service quality for all
- **Article 15 (Non-Discrimination):** Zero tolerance for bias on caste, religion, gender, language, disability, region
- **Article 21 (Life and Liberty):** Privacy protection, explainability, right to challenge AI decisions
- **Article 21A (Right to Education):** Universal access to AI literacy as a fundamental right

### 12.2 Equity Assurance

**Proactive Measures:**
- Targeted outreach to marginalized communities
- Removal of barriers (time, cost, language, accessibility)
- Safe and inclusive learning spaces
- Culturally appropriate content and facilitation
- Scholarships and support for economically disadvantaged

**Monitoring:**
- Regular tracking of demographic data
- Equity scorecard in monthly reports
- Community feedback on inclusivity
- Corrective actions if targets not met

### 12.3 Grievance Redress

**Three-Tier Mechanism:**
1. **Level 1:** NGO coordinator (5-day resolution)
2. **Level 2:** Government Partner nodal officer (10-day resolution)
3. **Level 3:** Joint Steering Committee (20-day resolution)

**Grievance Types:**
- Discrimination or harassment
- Quality or content issues
- Facilitator behavior
- Assessment or certification disputes
- CACF violations

**Grievance Channels:**
- In-person (NGO office, community meetings)
- Phone/SMS (toll-free number)
- Email
- Online portal (if accessible)
- Anonymity option for sensitive cases
