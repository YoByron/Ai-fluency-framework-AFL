## 1. BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

**WHEREAS** the Government of India has launched the AI Fluency Levels (AFL) Framework aligned with Viksit Bharat 2047 and National Education Policy (NEP) 2020;

**WHEREAS** the Educational Partner is committed to providing quality education and preparing students for the AI-driven future;

**WHEREAS** the Constitutional AI Compliance Framework (CACF) ensures equitable, rights-based AI deployment;

**WHEREAS** educational institutions are critical for building AI fluency among youth and educators;

**WHEREAS** both Parties recognize the mutual benefits of collaboration to integrate AFL into formal education;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, the Parties agree to collaborate as follows:

## 7. CURRICULUM INTEGRATION

### 7.1 Credit-Bearing Courses

**Course Structure:**

**Option A: Standalone AFL Course**
- Course Name: "AI Fluency for [Domain]"
- Credits: 2-4 credits
- Duration: 1 semester (30-60 contact hours)
- Assessment: Continuous assessment + AFL certification exam
- Grading: Letter grade based on AFL assessment scores

**Option B: AFL Module within Existing Course**
- Host Course: [e.g., Introduction to Computer Science, Digital Literacy]
- AFL Module: 10-20 hours within the course
- Credits: Partial credit (e.g., 1 credit out of 3-credit course)
- Assessment: AFL assessment + course exam

**Option C: Interdisciplinary AFL Program**
- Multiple courses across departments
- Total Credits: 6-12 credits
- Leads to AFL specialization/minor
- Capstone project integrating AI across domains

### 7.2 Learning Outcomes Mapping

**Alignment with Institutional Outcomes:**
- Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)
- Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)
- NEP 2020 competencies
- Industry skill requirements

**AFL-Specific Outcomes:**
- Demonstrate AI awareness and critical thinking (AFL-0, AFL-1)
- Apply AI tools for productivity and problem-solving (AFL-1, AFL-2)
- Integrate AI into domain-specific workflows (AFL-2, AFL-3)
- Lead AI adoption and innovation (AFL-3, AFL-4, AFL-5)

### 7.3 Pedagogy and Delivery

- Continuous assessment (40%): Assignments, quizzes, participation

## 8. FACULTY DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM

### 8.1 Faculty Training

**Training Pathways:**

**Tier 1: AFL Facilitator (40 hours)**
- Target: All faculty teaching AFL courses
- Training: Content mastery + pedagogy + platform
- Certification: AFL Facilitator certificate
- Benefit: Authorized to teach AFL-0

**Tier 2: AFL Trainer (80 hours)**
- Target: Senior faculty, department coordinators
- Training: Advanced content + mentoring + customization
- Certification: AFL Trainer certificate
- Benefit: Authorized to teach AFL-0, AFL-1; mentor other faculty

**Tier 3: AFL Master Trainer (120 hours)**
- Target: Faculty champions, researchers
- Training: Expert content + train-the-trainer + research
- Certification: AFL Master Trainer certificate
- Benefit: Authorized to teach all levels; train other faculty; lead research

### 8.2 Faculty Support

**Institutional Support:**
- Release time for training and facilitation
