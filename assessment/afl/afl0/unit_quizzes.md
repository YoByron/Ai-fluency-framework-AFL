## Unit 1 Quiz: AI in Your India (10 Questions)

### Question 1
**Which of the following is an example of AI in Indian financial services?**

A) Manual bank teller counting cash  
B) UPI fraud detection system  
C) Paper-based loan application  
D) Traditional fixed deposit account  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** UPI uses AI to detect fraudulent transactions by analyzing patterns in real-time. Manual processes and traditional banking don't use AI.

---

### Question 2
**Aadhaar authentication uses AI for:**

A) Only fingerprint recognition  
B) Only face recognition  
C) Biometric verification (fingerprint, iris, and face)  
D) Password verification  

**Correct Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Aadhaar uses AI-powered biometric verification across multiple modalities - fingerprint, iris scan, and facial recognition for secure authentication.

---

### Question 3
**Which government platform uses AI to recommend personalized learning content for students?**

A) IRCTC  
B) DIKSHA  
C) UPI  
D) Aadhaar  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** DIKSHA (Digital Infrastructure for Knowledge Sharing) uses AI to personalize learning content for 40 million+ students across India.

---

### Question 4
**How does AI learn to recognize patterns?**

A) By being programmed with specific rules  
B) By studying many examples (data) and finding patterns  
C) By copying human brain exactly  
D) By random guessing  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** AI learns through machine learning - studying large amounts of data (examples) to identify patterns, similar to how humans learn from experience.

---

### Question 5
**Which statement about AI is TRUE?**

A) AI is always 100% accurate  
B) AI can understand context like humans  
C) AI makes mistakes and has limitations  
D) AI will take over the world  

**Correct Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** AI is a tool with limitations - it makes mistakes, especially with new situations, biased data, or unusual contexts. It's not perfect and needs human oversight.

---

### Question 6
**The Kisan Suvidha app uses AI to help farmers with:**

A) Only weather forecasts  
B) Weather, crop advisory, and market prices  
C) Banking services  
D) Social media  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Kisan Suvidha provides AI-powered weather forecasts, crop advisory based on local conditions, and market price predictions to help farmers make informed decisions.

---

### Question 7

A) Helper AI (assistants, translators)  
B) Creative AI (image generators, writing aids)

A) Cure the disease  
B) Schedule vaccinations and predict demand

---

### Question 10
**Which statement about AI and jobs is most accurate?**

A) AI will take all jobs immediately

## Unit 2 Quiz: Using AI Safely and Effectively (10 Questions)

### Question 11
**When using a voice assistant in Hindi, what should you check first?**

A) If it's expensive  
B) Privacy settings and what data it collects  
C) If it can cook food  
D) If it works in English only  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Always review privacy settings first - check what data the voice assistant collects, stores, and shares. This protects your personal information.

---

### Question 12
**If an AI tool asks for access to your contacts, camera, and location, you should:**

A) Always grant all permissions  
B) Deny all permissions  
C) Review if each permission is necessary for the app's function  
D) Ignore the request  

**Correct Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Only grant permissions that are necessary for the app to function. For example, a camera app needs camera access, but may not need your contacts or location.

---

### Question 13
**You're using AI to create a family budget. The AI suggests cutting your food expenses by 80%. You should:**

A) Follow the AI advice immediately  
B) Verify if this is realistic and safe for your family  
C) Ignore AI completely  
D) Blame the AI for bad advice  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Always verify AI suggestions, especially for important decisions. An 80% cut in food expenses is likely unrealistic and potentially harmful. Use AI as a tool, not a replacement for judgment.

---

### Question 14
**When using AI to translate a government notice from English to your regional language, you should:**

A) Trust the translation 100%  
B) Cross-check important details with someone who knows both languages  
C) Assume it's always wrong  
D) Never use AI for translation  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** AI translation is helpful but not perfect, especially for official documents. Always verify important details, particularly for legal or government notices.

---

### Question 15
**You created a rangoli design using AI image generator. When sharing on social media, you should:**

A) Claim you drew it yourself  
B) Mention "Created with AI assistance"  
C) Not share AI-generated content  
D) Say a famous artist made it  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Be transparent about AI use. Mentioning AI assistance is ethical and honest, while claiming full credit or false attribution is misleading.

---

### Question 16
**If AI gives you wrong information about a health remedy, you should:**

A) Try the remedy anyway  
B) Fact-check with reliable sources (doctor, government health website)  
C) Share the wrong information with others  
D) Stop using all AI tools  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Always fact-check AI health information with qualified medical professionals or trusted sources like government health websites. Never rely solely on AI for health decisions.

---

### Question 17
**Your banking app's AI chatbot asks for your PIN and password. You should:**

A) Provide them immediately  
B) Never share PIN/password with any chatbot or AI  
C) Share only the PIN  
D) Share only the password  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Legitimate banks NEVER ask for PIN or password through chatbots or any other channel. This is likely a scam. Never share sensitive credentials with AI or anyone.

---

### Question 18
**You want to use AI to help your child with homework. The best approach is:**

A) Let AI do all the homework  
B) Use AI to explain concepts and guide learning, child does the work  
C) Copy AI answers directly  
D) Avoid AI completely  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** AI should support learning, not replace it. Use AI to explain difficult concepts, provide examples, and guide understanding, but the child should do the actual work to learn.

---

### Question 19
**An AI tool is not available in your regional language. You should:**

A) Give up on using AI  
B) Use Google Translate or look for regional alternatives  
C) Force yourself to use English  
D) Complain but do nothing  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Use translation tools as a bridge, look for AI tools in your language, and provide feedback to developers. Many AI tools are expanding language support based on user demand.

---

### Question 20
**Best practice when using AI tools:**

A) Share all your personal information for better results  
B) Use AI without thinking  
C) Verify AI outputs, protect privacy, use as a tool not replacement  
D) Never question AI decisions  

**Correct Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Always verify AI information, protect your privacy by limiting data sharing, and use AI as a helpful tool while maintaining your own judgment and critical thinking.

## Unit 3 Quiz: Thinking Critically About AI (10 Questions)

### Question 21
**AI says "Turmeric cures cancer." How should you verify this?**

A) Believe it immediately  
B) Check government health websites and medical journals  
C) Share with everyone  
D) Ignore all AI health advice  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Cross-reference with authoritative sources like government health websites, peer-reviewed medical journals, and consult doctors. Turmeric has health benefits but doesn't cure cancer.

---

### Question 22
**An AI job recommendation system suggests only men for technical roles and only women for administrative roles. This is an example of:**

A) Accurate AI  
B) Gender bias in AI  
C) Efficient AI  
D) Perfect AI  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** This is gender bias - the AI learned from historical data where men dominated technical roles. This perpetuates discrimination and violates CACF Article 15 (Non-Discrimination).

---

### Question 23
**You notice AI translation works well for Hindi-English but poorly for Odia-English. This suggests:**

A) Odia is a difficult language  
B) AI has language bias due to less training data for Odia  
C) AI is perfect  
D) Hindi is superior  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** AI performance depends on training data. More Hindi-English data exists than Odia-English, leading to better Hindi translation. This is a bias that needs addressing through more diverse data.

---

### Question 24
**An AI credit scoring system denies loans to people from certain regions. You should:**

A) Accept it as fair  
B) Report this as potential bias and discrimination  
C) Move to a different region  
D) Stop applying for loans  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Regional discrimination violates CACF Article 15. This bias should be reported to the lender and CACF portal. AI should evaluate creditworthiness based on individual factors, not region.

---

### Question 25
**When fact-checking AI-generated news, you should:**

A) Trust the AI completely  
B) Check the original sources and multiple reliable news outlets  
C) Assume all AI news is fake  
D) Only read one source  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Always verify news by checking original sources, cross-referencing with multiple reliable outlets, and looking for corroboration from trusted journalists and fact-checkers.

---

### Question 26
**AI facial recognition is less accurate for people with darker skin tones. This is:**

A) Acceptable  
B) A serious bias that can lead to false arrests and discrimination  
C) Not important  
D) Impossible to fix  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** This is a critical bias that has led to false arrests and discrimination. It violates CACF Article 15 and must be fixed through diverse training data and rigorous testing.

---

### Question 27
**You see AI-generated content that stereotypes your community. You should:**

A) Ignore it  
B) Document and report it to the service provider and CACF portal  
C) Share it widely  
D) Accept stereotypes as normal  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Stereotyping violates CACF principles. Document the bias (screenshots), report to the service provider, and file a CACF complaint if not resolved. Collective reporting can drive change.

---

### Question 28
**An AI app collects your location data every minute. To protect privacy, you should:**

A) Allow it without question  
B) Review if this is necessary and disable if not needed  
C) Uninstall all apps  
D) Share location with everyone  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Constant location tracking is often unnecessary and invasive. Review the app's need for location, disable background tracking, or use location only when app is active.

---

### Question 29
**AI shows you only news that matches your existing beliefs. This creates:**

A) Perfect information  
B) Filter bubble that limits diverse perspectives  
C) Better understanding  
D) Complete truth  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Filter bubbles limit exposure to diverse viewpoints, creating echo chambers. Actively seek different perspectives and sources to avoid this AI-driven bias.

---

### Question 30
**Best way to identify AI bias:**

A) Assume all AI is biased  
B) Check if AI treats different groups unfairly or makes stereotypical assumptions  
C) Trust AI completely  
D) Never use AI  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Look for unfair treatment of different demographic groups, stereotypical assumptions, exclusion of certain communities, or perpetuation of historical discrimination.

## Unit 4 Quiz: AI, Democracy, and Your Rights (10 Questions)

### Question 31
**Which article of the Indian Constitution guarantees equality in AI services?**

A) Article 10  
B) Article 14  
C) Article 25  
D) Article 50  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Article 14 (Equality Before Law) ensures all citizens receive equal access and quality in AI services, regardless of caste, religion, gender, language, region, or economic status.

---

### Question 32
**CACF stands for:**

A) Computer AI Compliance Framework  
B) Constitutional AI Compliance Framework  
C) Central AI Control Framework  
D) Corporate AI Certification Framework  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** CACF is the Constitutional AI Compliance Framework - India's unique approach to ensuring AI systems respect constitutional rights and democratic values.

---

### Question 33
**If an AI system violates your privacy, which constitutional right is violated?**

A) Article 14 (Equality)  
B) Article 15 (Non-Discrimination)  
C) Article 21 (Right to Life and Personal Liberty)  
D) Article 21A (Right to Education)  

**Correct Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Article 21 includes the right to privacy. AI systems must protect your personal data and cannot use it without consent.

---

### Question 34
**The first step in CACF grievance redress is:**

A) File a court case  
B) Complain to service provider (7 days)  
C) Post on social media  
D) Do nothing  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The 3-tier system starts with the service provider (Level 1, 7 days), then CACF portal (Level 2, 14 days), then legal system (Level 3) if needed.

---

### Question 35
**Article 21A guarantees:**

A) Right to vote  
B) Right to AI literacy as fundamental right  
C) Right to property  
D) Right to travel  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Article 21A (Right to Education) has been interpreted to include AI literacy as a fundamental right in the digital age, ensuring no one is left behind.

---

### Question 36
**An AI system denies you a job without explanation. Under CACF, you have the right to:**

A) Accept the decision  
B) Demand explanation and challenge the decision  
C) Give up on jobs  
D) Nothing  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Article 21 guarantees right to explainability. You can demand to know why AI made a decision affecting you and challenge it if unfair or biased.

---

### Question 37
**CoWIN platform scheduled 2 billion+ COVID vaccinations using AI. This is an example of AI in:**

A) Private business  
B) Public service delivery and governance  
C) Entertainment  
D) Sports  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** CoWIN demonstrates AI's role in efficient public service delivery - scheduling, demand prediction, inventory management, and equitable distribution.

---

### Question 38
**To participate in AI policy discussions, you can:**

A) Only vote in elections  
B) Submit feedback on MyGov, attend consultations, write to representatives  
C) Do nothing  
D) Only complain on social media  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Democratic participation includes submitting comments on draft policies (MyGov), attending public consultations, writing to elected representatives, and organized advocacy.

---

### Question 39
**If a company violates CACF and doesn't respond to your complaint, you should:**

A) Give up  
B) File complaint on CACF portal (cacf.gov.in)  
C) Accept the violation  
D) Only post on social media  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** If Level 1 (service provider) doesn't resolve your complaint in 7 days, escalate to Level 2 (CACF portal) for investigation and enforcement.

---

### Question 40
**CACF penalties for AI violations can be:**

A) Nothing  
B) ₹10 lakh to ₹5 crore fines, plus license revocation for repeated violations  
C) Just a warning  
D) Only ₹100 fine  

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** CACF has teeth - minor violations: ₹10-50 lakh, major violations: ₹50 lakh-₹5 crore, repeated violations: license revocation. This ensures accountability.
