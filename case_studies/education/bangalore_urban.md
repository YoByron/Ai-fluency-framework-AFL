 # Bangalore Urban (Karnataka) — Education/Governance Pilot (AFL-0/1/2)
 
 ## Context
 - Tech-forward urban district; program period: 2024–2025
 - Cohort: 50,000 citizens (AFL-0: 30k, AFL-1: 15k, AFL-2: 5k)
 
 ## Problem
 - Low individual AI utilization despite high enterprise adoption; need for educator and official upskilling
 
 ## Intervention (AFL Levels)
 - AFL-0 awareness at community and school hubs
 - AFL-1 interaction for teachers, officials, SMEs
 - AFL-2 integration for departmental workflows
 
 ## Implementation
 - Partners: State IT/Skill, DIKSHA; corporate volunteers for facilitation
 - Delivery: Online-first; English sufficient for ~85%
 - Curriculum: AFL-0/1 core; AFL-2 workflow projects
 - Assessment: Online quizzes, practical tasks, integration projects
 - Modules referenced: curriculum/sectors/education/modules/{personalized_learning.md,assessment_support.md,admin_efficiency.md}
 
 ## Results
 - Completion: 72% (AFL-0), 58% (AFL-1), 45% (AFL-2)
 - Productivity gains: 18% avg for AFL-1 graduates
 - Cost per participant: ₹180 (AFL-0), ₹420 (AFL-1), ₹6,500 (AFL-2)
 
 ## CACF Compliance
 - Real-time checks embedded in AFL-2 workflows; no critical flags reported
 
 ## Lessons Learned
 - High initial motivation; sustained engagement mechanisms needed
 - Corporate partnerships crucial for AFL-2
 
 ## Next Steps
 - Expand AFL-2 to additional departments; add vernacular content to lift AFL-1 completion
