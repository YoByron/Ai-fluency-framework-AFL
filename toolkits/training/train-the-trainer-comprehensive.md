## 1. Program Overview

### 1.1 Vision and Objectives

**Vision:** Create a nationwide network of 100,000+ certified AFL facilitators by 2030, capable of reaching 500 million citizens.

**Objectives:**
1. **Quality:** Train facilitators to deliver high-quality, engaging, and effective AFL programs
2. **Scale:** Build capacity to reach millions of learners across diverse geographies and demographics
3. **Equity:** Ensure facilitators can serve underserved populations (rural, women, SC/ST/OBC, low-literacy)
4. **CACF Compliance:** Embed constitutional AI principles in all facilitation
5. **Sustainability:** Create self-sustaining facilitator communities with peer learning and mentoring

### 1.2 Target Facilitators

**Primary Sources:**
- School and college teachers (40%)
- Government IT trainers (PMGDISHA, Skill India) (20%)
- Corporate volunteers (20%)
- NGO staff and community workers (15%)
- Independent trainers and consultants (5%)

**Annual Targets:**
- **Year 1 (2026):** 10,000 facilitators (5,000 AFL-0, 3,000 AFL-1, 2,000 AFL-2)
- **Year 2 (2027):** 25,000 facilitators (cumulative)
- **Year 3 (2028):** 50,000 facilitators (cumulative)
- **Year 4-5 (2029-2030):** 100,000 facilitators (cumulative)

### 1.3 Program Structure

**Three-Tier Certification:**

| Tier | AFL Level Authorized to Teach | Training Duration | Prerequisites |
|------|-------------------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Tier 1: AFL Facilitator** | AFL-0 only | 40 hours (1 week) | Bachelor's degree, digital literacy |
| **Tier 2: AFL Trainer** | AFL-0, AFL-1 | 80 hours (2 weeks) | AFL-1 certified + Tier 1 facilitation |
| **Tier 3: AFL Master Trainer** | AFL-0, AFL-1, AFL-2 | 120 hours (3 weeks) | AFL-2 certified + Tier 2 facilitation + 6 months experience |

**Specialized Tracks:**
- Rural & Low-Connectivity Facilitation
- Women's Empowerment & Gender-Sensitive Facilitation
- Sector-Specific Facilitation (Agriculture, MSMEs, Healthcare, etc.)
- Multilingual & Accessibility Facilitation

## 2. Facilitator Competency Framework

### 2.1 Core Competencies (All Tiers)

**1. Content Mastery**
- Deep understanding of AFL framework and philosophy
- Proficiency in AI concepts at the level being taught
- Ability to explain complex concepts in simple terms
- Knowledge of real-world AI applications and examples

**2. Pedagogical Skills**
- Adult learning principles and andragogy
- Facilitation techniques (not lecturing)
- Questioning and discussion management
- Hands-on activity design and delivery
- Assessment and feedback

**3. CACF Integration**
- Understanding of constitutional AI principles
- Ability to identify and address bias
- Equity-focused facilitation (gender, caste, language, region)
- Rights-based approach to AI literacy

**4. Technology Proficiency**
- Comfortable with AFL platform (LMS, assessment tools)
- Ability to troubleshoot basic technical issues
- Proficiency with AI tools being taught (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.)
- Offline and low-connectivity adaptations

**5. Communication & Interpersonal Skills**
- Clear and engaging communication
- Active listening and empathy
- Cultural sensitivity and local language proficiency
- Conflict resolution and grievance handling

**6. Professional Ethics**
- Commitment to learner success
- Integrity and accountability
- Continuous learning and improvement
- Collaboration and knowledge sharing

### 2.2 Tier-Specific Competencies

**Tier 1 (AFL Facilitator):**
- Deliver structured AFL-0 curriculum
- Manage groups of 20-30 participants
- Conduct basic assessments
- Provide participant support

**Tier 2 (AFL Trainer):**
- All Tier 1 competencies
- Deliver AFL-1 with hands-on practice
- Customize content for sectors and contexts
- Mentor Tier 1 facilitators
- Conduct impact assessments

**Tier 3 (AFL Master Trainer):**
- All Tier 1 and Tier 2 competencies
- Deliver AFL-2 with advanced integration
- Train and certify new facilitators (Tier 1 and Tier 2)
- Develop new content and modules
- Lead facilitator communities
- Conduct research and innovation

## 3. Certification Pathways

### 3.1 Tier 1: AFL Facilitator Certification

**Eligibility:**
- Bachelor's degree (any field)
- Digital literacy (comfortable with computers and smartphones)
- Fluency in local language(s)
- Commitment to 40-hour training + 3-6 months facilitation

**Training Components:**

**Part A: Content Mastery (16 hours)**
- AFL framework overview (2 hours)
- AFL-0 Module 1: What is AI? (4 hours)
- AFL-0 Module 2: AI in Indian Life (4 hours)
- AFL-0 Module 3: Rights and Safety (CACF) (4 hours)
- AFL-0 Module 4: Future Readiness (2 hours)

**Part B: Pedagogy & Facilitation (16 hours)**
- Adult learning principles (4 hours)
- Facilitation techniques and tools (4 hours)
- Handling diverse learners (4 hours)
- Assessment and feedback (4 hours)

**Part C: Practice & Certification (8 hours)**
- Mock facilitation sessions (4 hours)
- Peer feedback and improvement (2 hours)
- Certification assessment (2 hours)

**Certification Assessment:**
- Written exam: 50 MCQs on AFL-0 content and pedagogy (70% passing)
- Practical demo: 20-minute facilitation session (rubric-based, 70% passing)
- Peer evaluation: Feedback from 3 peers (qualitative)

**Certification Validity:** 2 years (renewable with 20 hours CPD)

---

### 3.2 Tier 2: AFL Trainer Certification

**Eligibility:**
- Tier 1 AFL Facilitator certified
- AFL-1 participant certification
- Minimum 3 months facilitation experience (20+ sessions)
- Positive participant feedback (NPS 4.0+)

**Training Components:**

**Part A: Advanced Content Mastery (24 hours)**
- AFL-1 Module 1: Effective Prompting (6 hours)
- AFL-1 Module 2: Critical Evaluation (6 hours)
- AFL-1 Module 3: Productivity Applications (6 hours)
- AFL-1 Module 4: Sector-Specific Deep Dives (6 hours)

**Part B: Advanced Pedagogy (24 hours)**
- Experiential and hands-on learning (6 hours)
- Sector-specific customization (6 hours)
- Mentoring and coaching skills (6 hours)
- Impact measurement and case study development (6 hours)

**Part C: Facilitation Practicum (24 hours)**
- Co-facilitate AFL-1 session with Master Trainer (8 hours)
- Independent AFL-1 facilitation (observed) (8 hours)
- Feedback and improvement (4 hours)
- Certification assessment (4 hours)

**Part D: Mentorship (8 hours)**
- Observe and mentor 2 Tier 1 facilitators
- Provide constructive feedback
- Document lessons learned

**Certification Assessment:**
- Written exam: 75 MCQs on AFL-1 content and advanced pedagogy (75% passing)
- Practical demo: 30-minute AFL-1 facilitation with hands-on activity (75% passing)
- Mentorship portfolio: Documentation of mentoring 2 Tier 1 facilitators
- Participant impact: Evidence of productivity improvement in learners

**Certification Validity:** 3 years (renewable with 30 hours CPD)

---

### 3.3 Tier 3: AFL Master Trainer Certification

**Eligibility:**
- Tier 2 AFL Trainer certified
- AFL-2 participant certification
- Minimum 6 months facilitation experience (50+ sessions)
- Demonstrated impact (case studies, productivity gains)
- Recommendation from 2 Master Trainers or program coordinators

**Training Components:**

**Part A: Expert Content Mastery (32 hours)**
- AFL-2 Module 1: Workflow Integration (8 hours)
- AFL-2 Module 2: Multi-Agent Orchestration (8 hours)
- AFL-2 Module 3: Custom Solutions (8 hours)
- AFL-2 Module 4: Organizational Change Management (8 hours)

**Part B: Train-the-Trainer Mastery (32 hours)**
- Facilitator training design and delivery (8 hours)
- Competency-based assessment and certification (8 hours)
- Curriculum development and innovation (8 hours)
- Research methods and impact evaluation (8 hours)

**Part C: Leadership & Community Building (32 hours)**
- Facilitator community leadership (8 hours)
- Peer learning network facilitation (8 hours)
- Stakeholder engagement and partnership building (8 hours)
- Thought leadership and knowledge dissemination (8 hours)

**Part D: Master Practicum (24 hours)**
- Train and certify 5 Tier 1 facilitators (16 hours)
- Co-develop new content or sector module (8 hours)

**Certification Assessment:**
- Written exam: 100 MCQs on AFL-2 content, train-the-trainer, and leadership (80% passing)
- Practical demo: 45-minute AFL-2 facilitation + 30-minute facilitator training session (80% passing)
- Innovation project: New content module, research paper, or impact study
- Facilitator training portfolio: Evidence of training 5 Tier 1 facilitators
- Community leadership: Demonstrated leadership in facilitator community

**Certification Validity:** 5 years (renewable with 50 hours CPD)

## 8. Quality Assurance

### 8.1 Facilitator Performance Monitoring

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Enrollment** | 100% of assigned target | Platform data |
| **Completion Rate** | 65% (AFL-0), 55% (AFL-1), 50% (AFL-2) | Platform data |
| **Assessment Scores** | 70% average | Platform data |
| **Participant Satisfaction** | NPS 4.0/5.0 | Post-training survey |
| **Equity** | 40% women, 25% rural, 25% SC/ST/OBC | Platform data |
| **CACF Compliance** | 90%+ | Compliance audit |
| **Attendance** | 80%+ session attendance | Platform data |

**Performance Review Frequency:**
- Monthly: Automated dashboard review
- Quarterly: Comprehensive performance review
- Annual: Impact assessment and certification renewal

### 8.2 Quality Audits

**Observation and Feedback:**
- Random observation of 10% of sessions
- Structured observation rubric
- Constructive feedback and coaching
- Follow-up observation for improvement

**Participant Feedback:**
- Post-session feedback (every session)
- Post-program feedback (end of program)
- Follow-up impact survey (3 months after)
- Grievance mechanism for issues

**Peer Review:**
- Peer observation (quarterly)
- Peer feedback and learning
- Best practice sharing

### 8.3 Remediation and Support

**Performance Improvement Plan (PIP):**

**Triggers for PIP:**
- Completion rate < 50%
- Participant satisfaction < 3.5/5.0
- Multiple participant complaints
- CACF violations
- Attendance < 70%

**PIP Process:**
1. Performance review meeting with Master Trainer
2. Root cause analysis
3. Improvement plan with specific actions and timeline
4. Enhanced support and mentoring
5. Follow-up observation and review
6. Certification suspension if no improvement after 3 months

**Facilitator Support:**
- Additional training and coaching
- Peer mentoring
- Reduced participant load
- Co-facilitation with experienced facilitator
- Access to counseling and support services

## Appendix C: Sample Lesson Plan (AFL-0, Module 1, Session 1)

**Topic:** What is AI? - Introduction

**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define AI in simple terms
- Identify 3 examples of AI in daily life
- Distinguish between AI and non-AI technologies

**Materials:**
- Presentation deck
- Smartphones (participant devices)
- Flipchart and markers
- Handout: "AI in My Life"

**Lesson Flow:**

**1. Icebreaker (10 min)**
- Participants share one technology they use daily
- Facilitator notes on flipchart

**2. What is AI? (20 min)**
- Simple definition: "Machines that can learn and make decisions"
- Examples: Voice assistants, recommendations, translations
- Non-examples: Calculators, alarm clocks (no learning)
- Interactive poll: "Is this AI?" (5 scenarios)

**3. Hands-On Demo (30 min)**
- Voice assistant (Google Assistant, Siri): Ask questions, set reminders
- Google Translate: Translate sentences
- Google Photos: Search for objects, faces
- Participants try on their phones

**4. Group Discussion (20 min)**
- "Where have you seen AI in your life?"
- Participants share examples
- Facilitator categorizes: Home, work, government, entertainment

**5. Wrap-Up (10 min)**
- Recap key points
- Preview next session
- Homework: Find 3 more AI examples in your life

**Assessment:**
- Participation in discussion
- Successful completion of hands-on demo
- Homework submission
