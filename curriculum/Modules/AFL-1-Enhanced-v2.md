## Unit 2: Critical AI Evaluation (10 hours)

### 2.1 Hallucination Detection (3 hours)

**What are Hallucinations?**
AI "making up" facts, citations, statistics, or events that don't exist.

**Common Patterns:**
- Fake academic citations
- Invented statistics and numbers
- False attributions and quotes
- Fabricated historical events
- Non-existent products or companies

**Detection Techniques:**
1. **Cross-reference:** Verify claims with multiple sources
2. **Source check:** Look up cited sources directly
3. **Logical consistency:** Does it make sense?
4. **Domain knowledge:** Does it align with what you know?
5. **Confidence indicators:** Note when AI uses hedging language

**Practice Exercise:**
```
AI Output: "According to a 2024 study by IIT Delhi, 87% of 
Indian farmers using AI crop prediction tools increased 
yields by 34%. The study, published in the Journal of 
Agricultural AI, surveyed 15,000 farmers across 12 states."

Task: Identify potential hallucinations

AI Coach Guidance:
- Does this study exist? (Search for it)
- Is "Journal of Agricultural AI" real?
- Are the statistics plausible?
- Can you find the original source?

Answer: Likely hallucinated - no such study found, journal 
doesn't exist, statistics too precise without source.
```

**20 Practice Scenarios:** Identify hallucinations in AI outputs across domains

### 2.2 Fact-Checking and Verification (3 hours)

**Systematic Verification Process:**
1. Identify factual claims
2. Find primary sources
3. Cross-reference multiple sources
4. Check publication dates
5. Assess source credibility
6. Document findings

**Tools and Techniques:**
- Google Scholar for academic claims
- Government databases for statistics
- News aggregators for current events
- Expert consultation for specialized topics
- Fact-checking websites (Snopes, FactCheck.org, Alt News)

**Indian Context Examples:**
- Verifying government scheme details
- Checking agricultural statistics
- Validating health information
- Confirming legal/regulatory information

**10 Practice Tasks:** Verify AI-generated claims about Indian topics

### 2.3 Bias Identification (2 hours)

**Types of Bias:**
- **Selection bias:** What's included/excluded
- **Confirmation bias:** Favoring certain viewpoints
- **Cultural bias:** Western-centric or urban-centric
- **Temporal bias:** Outdated information
- **Representation bias:** Who's represented, who's not

**Detection Methods:**
- Check for balanced perspectives
- Identify missing viewpoints
- Assess cultural appropriateness
- Verify demographic representation
- Question assumptions

**Practice:** Analyze 10 AI outputs for bias

### 2.4 Quality Assessment Framework (2 hours)

**Evaluation Rubric:**
- **Accuracy:** Factually correct? (0-10)
- **Relevance:** Addresses the question? (0-10)
- **Completeness:** Covers all aspects? (0-10)
- **Clarity:** Easy to understand? (0-10)
- **Bias:** Balanced and fair? (0-10)
- **Currency:** Up-to-date information? (0-10)
- **Actionability:** Provides useful guidance? (0-10)

**Create Personal Checklist:**
Learners develop domain-specific quality criteria

**Practice:** Evaluate 20 AI outputs using rubric

## Unit 4: Ethical AI Usage & CACF (6 hours)

### 4.1 Constitutional AI Compliance (3 hours)

**CACF Principles in Practice:**

**Article 14 (Equality):**
- Ensure AI tools accessible to all
- No discrimination in AI usage
- Equal opportunity to benefit from AI

**Article 15 (Non-Discrimination):**
- Identify bias in AI outputs
- Avoid perpetuating stereotypes
- Ensure fair representation

**Article 19 (Freedom of Speech):**
- Use AI responsibly, not for misinformation
- Respect others' rights while using AI
- Attribute AI assistance appropriately

**Article 21 (Privacy):**
- Minimize data sharing with AI tools
- Protect personal and community information
- Use privacy-preserving practices

**Article 21A (Right to Education):**
- Use AI to enhance learning, not replace it
- Ensure AI benefits reach all learners
- Support inclusive education

**Practical Scenarios (10):**
- Evaluating AI hiring tools for bias
- Protecting privacy while using AI health tools
- Ensuring fair AI credit scoring
- Using AI for inclusive education

### 4.2 Privacy & Security (2 hours)

**Data Minimization:**
- Share only necessary information
- Use anonymized data when possible
- Avoid sensitive personal details

**Secure AI Tool Selection:**
- Check privacy policies
- Prefer on-device AI when possible
- Use reputable providers
- Understand data retention

**Best Practices:**
- Don't share: Aadhaar, bank details, passwords
- Be cautious with: Health data, location, photos
- Safe to share: General questions, public information

### 4.3 Attribution & Academic Integrity (1 hour)

**When to Attribute AI:**
- Academic work: Always disclose AI assistance
- Professional work: Follow organizational policy
- Creative work: Transparent about AI collaboration

**How to Attribute:**
```
"This report was prepared with assistance from AI tools 
(ChatGPT, Gemini) for research, drafting, and editing. 
All facts were verified and final content is author's 
responsibility."
```
