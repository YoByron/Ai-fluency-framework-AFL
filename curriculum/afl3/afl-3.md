# AFL-3 Curriculum: AI Orchestration (Strategic Implementers)

## Objective
Prepare leaders to architect multi-agent/multi-tool systems, govern enterprise-wide AI adoption, and institutionalize CACF compliance.

## Target Audience
- Senior managers, architects, policymakers, L&D heads

## Duration
80–120 hours (executive/blended; capstone required)

## Core Competencies
1. Multi-agent/system architecture and integration patterns
2. Program/portfolio strategy and ROI modeling
3. Governance at scale (risk, CACF, audit, grievance)
4. Change leadership and capability building

## Module Breakdown
- Module 1: Orchestration Patterns & Reference Architectures (20–30h)
- Module 2: Strategy, Roadmaps & ROI (20–30h)
- Module 3: Governance, Risk & Compliance at Scale (20–30h)
- Module 4: Transformation & Talent Systems (20–30h)

## Learning Activities
- Design an enterprise reference architecture (human-in-loop)
- Build a 3-year AI roadmap with quantified ROI and risks
- Draft an organizational CACF policy and escalation matrix
- Create a capability ladder (AFL-0→3) and rollout plan

## Assessment
- Capstone: Enterprise orchestration plan with ≥30% target efficiency
- Governance pack: CACF policy, audit plan, risk register
- Playbook: Organization-specific enablement and training plan

## Resources
- Architecture blueprints, governance templates, ROI calculator
- CACF APIs and audit-log schemas

## Certification
Certificate: “AI Orchestration (AFL-3)” — valid 3 years; recertification via portfolio review

## Implementation Notes
- Prioritize safety-critical workflows for human oversight
- Establish an internal review board for CACF and model risk
